## 1. Guest criteria — the three non-negotiables

All three must pass for any candidate. One fail = skip, do not draft outreach.

### Requirement 1 — Artisan trade

The artisan trades sit at the intersection of fine arts and skilled trades. The key test:

> **Does the work combine aesthetic/design skill with structural/functional making? Is the end product something people USE, not just look at?**

**IS an artisan trade (examples):**
- Ceramicists making functional dishware (not sculptors making ceramic statues)
- Furniture makers building functional pieces with design + structural skill
- Bladesmiths / knifemakers
- Glassblowers (functional or artistic glasswork)
- Shoemakers / cobblers / bootmakers
- Leather goods makers
- Chefs / bakers / culinary artisans
- Butchers
- Bookbinders
- Metalsmiths (non-jewelry: knives, hardware, architectural)
- Upholsterers / furniture restorers
- Textile workers / weavers
- Printmakers
- Stained glass artists
- Watchmakers / fine instrument repairers
- Neon sign makers
- Boat builders
- Any rare, hyperlocal, or disappearing trade

**IS NOT an artisan trade:**
- Fine art: painters, sculptors, photographers, illustrators
- Construction trades: plumbers, electricians, house painters, general contractors
- Jewelry: lower priority — only if genuinely exceptional and distinct from Season 1
- Hobbyists or part-time makers (see Requirement 2)

### Requirement 2 — Established & successful artisan

This is their livelihood, not a hobby. Full-time in their craft.

**Success indicators (one or more of):**
- ~10,000+ combined social media following (personal + brand accounts; wiggle room — 3K personal + 50K brand = yes)
- Awards, press features, or notable recognition (James Beard Award, Netflix appearance, major publication features)
- Multiple locations or established business presence
- Strong community reputation / word-of-mouth recognition

**Why this matters:** Season 1 showcased artisans at various stages, which unintentionally perpetuated the "starving artist" narrative. Season 2+ deliberately features artisans who prove the trades can be a viable, thriving livelihood. **Expansive, inspiring stories — not hardship narratives.**

**Benchmark examples:**
- Stuart Brioza: James Beard Award winner, 3 restaurants, ~11K personal IG + restaurant accounts with massive combined reach
- Momoko Schafer: Netflix's *Blown Away* contestant, ~26K IG followers

### Requirement 3 — Location (San Francisco)

For Season 2, the artisan must be **based in and working out of San Francisco.** Non-negotiable. Their workshop/studio should be in SF.

**Exception precedent:** West Oakland is adjacent and has been included for Elias Sideris / Bernal Cutlery connection. SF proper is still the target.

**How to verify:**
- Website contact / about page
- Instagram tagged locations and bio
- Google Maps business listing
- Recent posts showing studio context

Do not assume SF based on an SF-adjacent handle or vibe. Confirm explicitly.

## 2. The Venn test

Every candidate gets run through this before outreach drafting. Both conditions must be yes:

**Condition A — Aesthetic + structural:**
Does the work combine aesthetic/design skill with structural/functional making?
- [ ] Yes — e.g., a ceramicist shaping a bowl that's both beautiful and drinkable from
- [ ] No — e.g., a painter making only paintings, or a plumber making only plumbing

**Condition B — Used, not just looked at:**
Is the end product something people use in their daily lives?
- [ ] Yes — plates, shoes, furniture, tools, bread, knives, clothing
- [ ] No — sculptures, wall art, decorative-only pieces

If both conditions are yes AND Requirements 2 & 3 above pass → Talent Scout drafts outreach.

If either condition is no or borderline → flag to Briana, do not draft.

## 3. Where to look — sourcing channels

### Primary sources (check first, highest yield)

- **Instagram** — Search by trade-specific hashtags + SF location tags. Look at follower count, content quality, whether they're clearly SF-based. Check bio for studio location.
- **TikTok** — Same trade + location searches. Growing channel for artisan discovery.
- **Google Search** — `"[specific trade] San Francisco"` — e.g., "ceramicist San Francisco," "furniture maker San Francisco," "bookbinder San Francisco." Follow through to personal websites and social profiles.

### Secondary sources

- **SF craft studios, guilds, and collectives:** SFMade members, SOMArts, The Crucible (adjacent), Workshop Residence
- **"Made in SF" product brands:** Solo makers or small teams behind locally-made goods
- **Press coverage:** SF Chronicle, Eater SF, 48 Hills, The Bold Italic, Mission Local — local media features of SF artisans
- **Etsy / marketplace sellers** — SF-based with strong followings
- **Referrals** — Artisans already in the network recommending peers (artisan-to-artisan warm intros)
- **In-person discovery** — Shops, studios, craft fairs, farmers markets in SF (the Bryr exemplar came from a shop visit)

### Scoring tiers for candidates found

- **Tier A — Priority draft:** Passes all three requirements unambiguously, has 10K+ combined following or a major recognition, has specific SF studio presence, aligns with open trade category
- **Tier B — Draft after validation:** Passes all three but needs verification on one dimension (location, following, professional status)
- **Tier C — Flag to Briana:** Borderline on Venn test, or trade already covered, or social following in the ~5–10K range and needs judgment call
- **Skip:** Fails any non-negotiable, is a past guest, is on declined list, or is outside Season 2 geographic scope

## 4. Validation checklist

Run for every candidate before outreach is drafted:

- [ ] Physically based in San Francisco (check website, social profiles, tagged posts)
- [ ] Trade qualifies per Venn test (both conditions yes)
- [ ] Combined social media following meets the bar (with wiggle room for awards/press)
- [ ] Their work has visual/narrative interest (would this make a compelling episode?)
- [ ] They appear to be full-time / professional (not a hobbyist)
- [ ] Press, awards, or notable recognition noted (if any)
- [ ] Contact info found — email preferred, IG DM as pathway otherwise
- [ ] Not already a past guest (check § 7 and episodes database)
- [ ] Not currently in the pipeline (check Notion Contacts DB, tag: Podcast Guest)
- [ ] Not on declined list (currently: Bryr Studio / Isobel Shofield)
- [ ] Trade category contribution to Season 2 diversity noted (not doubling up)

## 5. Outreach channels & priority

### Priority order

1. **Email** — Preferred primary channel when an email address is available. Check website contact page, social media bio, press mentions.
2. **Instagram DM** — Use as a pathway to email. If email isn't findable, DM to introduce briefly and request their email for a proper outreach.
3. **Through their team/shop** — If they have a brick-and-mortar or team (like the Bryr example), reach out to the team and ask to be connected. Respect the chain; don't go around it.
4. **TikTok DM** — Discovery + outreach channel, less proven but valid for TikTok-native artisans.

### Outreach email sender address

`briana@thetradesshowpod.com`

### What NOT to do

- Don't pitch on LinkedIn (artisans are rarely active there)
- Don't cold-DM on a platform where they haven't posted in 6+ months
- Don't email and DM simultaneously — pick one channel, give them a chance to respond
- Don't bypass a visible team/shop contact to find the founder's personal email

## 6. Outreach cadence

Matches Sponsorship Director and PR Director cadence for system-wide consistency.

### Touchpoint sequence — 3 touches max

#### Touch 1 — Initial outreach
- **When:** Tue–Thu preferred, 9am–11am PT
- **Channel:** Email (preferred) / IG DM / through team
- **Format:** Per voice.md structure — specific discovery story, 15-minute ask, exploratory framing
- **Length:** 100–180 words (email), 40–80 (DM), 100–160 (team)

#### Touch 2 — "What to expect" follow-up
- **When:** 7–10 business days after Touch 1, only if no response
- **Channel:** Email, reply on same thread
- **Format:** The proven bullet breakdown format (see Exemplar C in voice.md)
- **Content:** 15-30 min call + 3-hour shoot day + Spotify/YouTube promotion + SF narrative

#### Touch 3 — Final check-in (optional)
- **When:** 14 business days after Touch 2, only if no response
- **Channel:** Email, reply on same thread
- **Format:** 40–80 words, "last note from me," closes gracefully, leaves door open for future seasons

**After Touch 3:** Mark as "Passed / No Response" in Contacts DB. Do not re-pitch this season. Revisit in Season 3 planning if the category fit still makes sense.

### Post-response handoff

Once an artisan responds positively:
- Reply within 24 hours of their response
- If they ask for a call time, offer 2–3 calendar windows
- **Handoff to Briana for call scheduling and filming logistics.** Talent Scout does not coordinate shoot days, location logistics, or release timing.

### Frequency rules for the agent

- **Batch size per run:** 3 leads in TEST mode, 10 in FULL mode. (Smaller than Sponsorship/PR — artisan outreach needs extra care and the archive of exemplars is smaller.)
- **Sends per week, max:** 8 fresh outreach touches. More and Briana can't keep up with replies; voice quality slips.
- **Follow-ups per day, max:** 3 queued touches.
- **Time of day:** Tue–Thu, 9am–11am PT. Never Monday morning (inbox chaos) or weekends (not respecting their time off).

### Cadence exceptions

**Time-sensitive episode slots:** If a specific Season 2 slot has a target film date, accelerate the sequence — Touch 1 and Touch 2 can compress to 5 business days. Don't compress Touch 3.

**Warm intros (referrals from past guests):** Already-warm, don't follow the cold cadence. Reply in kind to whatever channel the intro was made in.

## 11. Season 2 narrative — the why-now framing

Season 2 is set entirely in San Francisco — the AI capital of the world. The tension between technology and handcraft is the throughline. This isn't anti-tech; it's about what endures alongside it.

**Why this matters for outreach:**

The SF narrative gives the "why you, why now" framing in every pitch:
> *You're not just being featured on a podcast — you're part of a deliberate story about San Francisco's artisan identity at a moment when that story has never been more relevant.*

Specific narrative moves Talent Scout can use:
- "Your work, in the heart of [SF neighborhood], feels quintessential San Francisco to me" (Danny exemplar pattern)
- "Season 2 set entirely in San Francisco" (stated plainly in every outreach)
- "Modern artisans in the AI capital of the world" (when appropriate — not every artisan cares about the tech angle)

**When to emphasize vs. when to soft-pedal the AI framing:**
- **Emphasize:** when the artisan's location (SOMA, Mission, Bernal, Outer Sunset, Dogpatch) carries SF character, or when their work has a technology-adjacent counterpoint
- **Soft-pedal:** when the artisan is pure-craft focused and might find the tech framing distracting. Lead with the craft; let SF stand on its own.
