## 1. Evaluation framework — the 6-point fit test

Every opportunity gets scored before any application work begins.

### The 6 points

1. **Non-dilutive.** Funder does not take equity, royalties, revenue share, or any other ownership-style claim. **Hard pass if dilutive.**
2. **Mission alignment.** Does the funder's stated mission align with the Slow Renaissance / artisan trades / craft / small business / tech-for-humanity themes?
3. **Eligibility match.** Entity type, location, revenue stage, demographics, business stage — does Briana qualify? (See § 7 for entity structure.)
4. **Effort-to-reward ratio.** Estimated hours of application work vs. award size. Use the tier table in § Core Principles of system-prompt.md.
5. **Timeline compatibility.** Deadline far enough out (or short enough) to fit current priorities. A 2-week deadline for a complex application may not clear.
6. **Funder reputation and legitimacy.** Has the funder been in public controversy? Is the award real? Is there any red flag (political alignment conflict, labor issues, values mismatch)?

### Scoring

- **6/6 pass:** priority evaluation, surface to Briana with "recommended"
- **5/6 pass (with the failing point being low-severity):** surface with the concern flagged
- **4/6 or fewer pass:** skip unless there's a very strong reason to surface
- **Non-dilutive = 0/6:** hard pass regardless of other points

### What to do if borderline

- Flag to Briana with the specific concern and recommendation
- Don't draft until she green-lights
- Example: *"This grant is a strong mission fit and the reward is $10K, but the application requires 501(c)(3) status. Artisanship LLC isn't formed and Briana Augustina LLC is a for-profit LLC — we'd be ineligible. Flagging."*

## 2. Venture funding angles — detailed

### The Trades Show / Artisanship

**Funding categories that fit:**
- **Arts and cultural preservation grants** — state arts councils, NEA, Humanities councils, heritage craft organizations (Heritage Crafts Red List-adjacent)
- **Media production grants** — documentary grants (IDA, ITVS, Firelight), podcast production grants (LPM/Google podcast fund when it returns, Spotify creator funds)
- **Small business / women-led business grants** — Hello Alice, Freelancing Females, IFundWomen, women-focused SBA tracks
- **Regional/local grants** — SFMade, SF Arts Commission, Bay Area arts councils
- **Craft-specific grants** — if they exist and align (e.g., Craft Emergency Relief Fund when active)

**Framing notes:**
- Lead with cultural preservation and documentation angle for arts grants
- Lead with small business + founder story for entrepreneurship grants
- Lead with the AI/tech tension for grants that care about the technology moment
- The Frank Beneduci and Angela Wilson moments are powerful proof for media-grant pitches

### The Corral

**Funding categories that fit:**
- **Workforce development grants** — federal + state workforce boards, skilled trades pipeline grants
- **Tech-for-good grants** — Mozilla, Omidyar, other funders of civic/labor tech
- **Small business infrastructure grants** — grants for tools that support entrepreneurs
- **Apprenticeship / vocational training grants** — where The Corral's matching pipeline is the value proposition
- **Regional economic development grants** — when the Corral serves regional employment

**Framing notes:**
- Lead with the 2.1M skilled trades workers needed by 2030 stat
- Ground in specific artisan examples + employer names (Tiffany & Co., Williams Sonoma, Hermès, Dandelion Chocolate)
- Name The Corral as infrastructure, not product
- The Reddit 50-signups day is a strong proof point for organic demand

### Artisanship Community (forthcoming — Heartbeat membership + The Dinner Party)

**What this is:**
A membership community (on Heartbeat) + flagship in-person event series (The Dinner Party — outdoor meals curated by and featuring artisan-made elements: ceramicist dishware, woodworker tables, florist arrangements, chef/baker creations). Connects three audience segments: artisans in practice, craft-curious, artisan admirers.

**Funding categories that fit:**
- **Community-building grants** — Heartbeat grant, community-platform funders, Luminate
- **Women-founder / community-first grants** — where the community-building angle is core
- **Arts + culture in-person gathering grants** — supper-club-style or artist-gathering funders
- **Regional hospitality / craft tourism grants** — if The Dinner Party scales to specific cities

**Framing notes:**
- Lead with the three-audience model (artisans, craft-curious, admirers)
- Use "peer isolation" and "audience separation" as coined concepts that name the community problem Artisanship solves
- The Dinner Party is tactile, specific, memorable — use it as the vision anchor in community-grant pitches
- This is the newest arm of Artisanship — don't overclaim existing community traction; frame as the natural next step from existing 2K+ engaged audience

### Detto / Slow Business Movement

**Funding categories that fit:**
- **AI/tech for humanity** — Mozilla, EFF-adjacent funders, digital wellness funders
- **Voice / accessibility grants** — tools that use voice as interface
- **Productivity / cognitive tools grants** — wellness-tech overlap
- **Solo founder / creator tools grants** — when they exist

**Framing notes:**
- Lead with the "technology that returns us to ourselves" thesis — not AI productivity
- Avoid positioning as productivity tool — position as cognitive/thinking tool
- Detto is early-stage — don't overclaim traction

### Neo-Renaissance Agent Ecosystem

**Funding categories that fit:**
- **Future of work grants** — funders interested in how AI changes entrepreneurship
- **Solo founder / small business AI adoption grants** — emerging category
- **Open source / platform grants** — if any part of the system becomes shareable

**Framing notes:**
- This is the most speculative venture in terms of funding — the story matters more than the traction
- Lead with the scalable-model thesis (this works for other solo founders too)
- Pairs well with founder-level fellowships (O'Shaughnessy)

### Cross-venture / founder-level fellowships

**Funding categories that fit:**
- **Fellowships that fund the person** — O'Shaughnessy Fellowship ($100K, end-April deadline), Echoing Green, Ashoka, Ashoka U, Emerson Collective Fellowship, Obama Foundation Fellowship
- **Builders / systems-thinker fellowships** — newer programs like the ones from Schmidt Futures, Roux Institute, etc.
- **Writer/creator fellowships** — if Briana's writing gets amplified (e.g., MacDowell, Yaddo — but these are usually time-away, not funding-per-se)

**Framing notes:**
- These fund *Briana as a builder*, not any specific product
- Lead with the connecting thread — how Artisanship + Detto + agents are all the same question from different angles
- This is where the cobbler story fits perfectly — it's a founder narrative

**Tech/AI-for-good:**

### Where NOT to scan

- **Venture capital databases** (Crunchbase, PitchBook) — dilutive
- **Angel investment platforms** — dilutive

- **Pitch competitions** for startup tracks — most offer prizes contingent on equity-fit or pipeline venture money
- **Convertible note / SAFE programs** — dilutive

### Scan cadence

- **Weekly (Monday 7am PT):** Scan all aggregators + category-specific sources for new postings in the last 7 days
- **Surface top 3-5 matches** in a weekly report to Briana
- **Flag any approaching deadlines** from tracked opportunities

## 8. Off-limits funders

### Hard no — always skip

- Any VC firm offering "grant programs" that are actually investment pipelines
- Any dilutive equity program
- Any pitch competition where the prize is investment contingent on terms
- Any funder whose money comes from values-conflicting sources (tobacco, weapons, extractive industries, predatory finance)
- Any program with a pay-to-apply fee over $50 (application fees above this are a signal)

### Flag before proceeding

- Any funder with active public controversy (labor issues, political alignment concerns, past recipient complaints)
- Any funder whose past awarded projects are values-misaligned
- Any funder new to the space with no track record

Currently empty. Grows as concerns surface.

When a new concern emerges, add to this list + update conflicts.md if it touches on broader business conflicts.

## 9. Deadline tracking

### Integration with Ops Chief

- Funding Scout maintains a running list of tracked opportunity deadlines
- Surface in Ops Chief daily briefing when a deadline is within 14 days
- Surface urgently when within 3 days

### Calendar integration

- Each tracked opportunity gets a Notion task: `"[Funder] — Application deadline"`, Task type, To-Do Date = 7 days before deadline (work start), Date field = deadline
- Venture: per the venture the application targets

### After a deadline passes

- If application was submitted: update status to "submitted," await outcome
- If not submitted: mark as "missed" and log reason (pivoted away, decided against, lost track — the last one is the Supervisor-flag-worthy case)

## 10. Founder-level fellowships — the O'Shaughnessy pattern

Some fellowships fund the person, not any specific product. Briana's cross-venture work is uniquely well-suited to these.

### The pattern

**What these fellowships want:**
- A person with a unifying thesis across multiple projects
- Evidence of building, not just theorizing
- A vision for what the fellowship enables

**What Briana offers:**
- Three projects that are "the same project from different angles" (Artisanship + Detto + agent ecosystem)
- A unifying thesis (Slow Renaissance — technology in service of humanity)
- Demonstrated building (Season 2 of The Trades Show, Detto waitlist, agent ecosystem operating)

### Canonical application (O'Shaughnessy, due late April 2026)

- **The frame:** fund the builder, not the product
- **The thesis:** "technology that returns us to ourselves" — the connecting thread across Artisanship, Detto, and the agent system
- **The Neo-Renaissance model:** the agent ecosystem as infrastructure that could work for other solo founders — a scalable model for human-centered building
- **What the fellowship enables:** a year to build the connective tissue and prove the model

### Other fellowships to watch

- Echoing Green — social entrepreneurship
- Emerson Collective Dial Fellowship
- Obama Foundation Fellowship (every few years, international cohort)
- Ashoka Fellowship (long-term, global)

For each, when a cycle opens, evaluate using the founder-level framing — not venture-specific framing.
