## Voice

### Core voice principles

1. **Genuine admiration is the foundation.** Reference specific pieces, techniques, or moments from their work. Show you actually looked. Flattery and generic praise don't work — specificity does.
2. **The discovery story matters.** Every pitch includes how Briana found them. A friend's recommendation, a shop visit, a Netflix show, a press piece. "I was looking into glass artisans in San Francisco and your work kept coming up" lands; "I found your Instagram" doesn't.
3. **Warm but brief on first touch.** Ask for 15 minutes. Not a commitment, not a filming date. Exploratory. "To see if it feels like a good fit."
4. **Credibility without bragging.** Link to `thetradesshowpod.com`. Name past guests when relevant. Mention YouTube + Spotify. Don't quote audience numbers — artisans care about editorial fit, not reach.
5. **Proof of humanity.** The `@brianaaugustina` IG handle reference is signature. "For proof I'm human" is an allowed, endearing phrasing that defuses the spam-detection reflex.
6. **Season 2 narrative frames the ask.** "Season 2 set in San Francisco" is the throughline. Their work, in this city, at this moment, is why they belong.

### Tonal signatures to preserve

- **"I'm Briana Ottoboni, host and creator of The Trades Show"** — signature opener, full name on first touch
- **"Warmly + with gratitude, Briana"** — signature sign-off, non-negotiable
- **`@brianaaugustina for proof I'm human`** — the human-proof IG reference, used on cold outreach
- **"See if it feels like a good fit"** — the exploratory framing for the 15-minute ask
- **"Would love to share your story"** — value statement, not transactional

### What every outreach does

- Names the artisan correctly (first name, spelled right) — double-check, especially across platforms where spellings vary
- Opens with a specific discovery story or genuine admiration of their work
- Positions The Trades Show in 1–2 sentences with the link
- Connects them to the Season 2 SF narrative
- Asks for a 15-minute call (never a filming commitment on Touch 1)
- Signs off "Warmly + with gratitude, Briana"
- Often includes `@brianaaugustina` as the "proof I'm human" handle

### What every outreach avoids

- Generic "I'd love to feature you on my podcast"
- PR-pitch or brand-deal tone
- Overly long on first touch (save the breakdown for follow-up)
- Any mention of compensation — there isn't any, don't draw attention to it
- Pushy or salesy language
- Overly formal register
- Broad "artisan" language when a specific trade noun exists (say "bladesmith," not "maker")

## Format

### Length

- **Cold email (Touch 1):** 100–180 words body. Shorter than press or sponsorship outreach — artisans are busy, working with their hands, not at a desk.
- **Instagram DM (intro):** 40–80 words. Request their email as the pathway; don't try to close in the DM.
- **Team / shop outreach:** 100–160 words. Polite, facilitator-respecting, includes social proof.
- **Follow-up (Touch 2 with breakdown):** 150–220 words. The "what to expect" is the value-add.
- **Final check-in (Touch 3):** 40–80 words. Shortest.

### Structure — Cold email (Touch 1)

```
[Subject line — specific and warm]

Hi [First name],

[Personal hook — 1–2 sentences. How you found them OR a specific piece/technique/moment you admire. Real and specific.]

[Who I am + what the show is — 1–2 sentences. Name, host of The Trades Show, link (thetradesshowpod.com), available on YouTube & Spotify.]

[Why them, why Season 2 — 1–2 sentences. Connect their work/location/story to the SF Season 2 narrative.]

[The ask — 1 sentence. "Would you be open to a quick 15 min call..." with exploratory framing.]

[Optional: scheduling link + flexibility offer, past guest names as social proof, upcoming Season 2 teases]

Looking forward to connecting!

Warmly + with gratitude,
Briana
```

### Structure — Instagram DM (intro only)

```
Hi [first name]! I'm Briana, host of The Trades Show (thetradesshowpod.com) — a conversation series with modern artisans on YouTube & Spotify.

[One sentence: how you found them or what specifically resonated]

I'm working on Season 2 set in San Francisco and would love to share your story on the show. What's the best email to send a proper intro to?

Warmly,
Briana (@brianaaugustina)
```

### Structure — Team / shop outreach

```
Hi [Shop/Team name] Team,

[Personal in-person or discovery hook — came into the shop, visited during [event], found through [specific path]]

I'm Briana, host of The Trades Show, a conversation series where I interview modern artisans (available on Spotify & YouTube). I would love to connect with [founder name] about being a guest on the show!

Would you be able to connect me with her/him/them directly? If there's another way I can get in touch, let me know :)

[Warm seasonal or business detail — "hope you are in the midst of a successful holiday season" etc.]

Warmly + with gratitude,
Briana

[Optional: past episode references — "Here are some of my favorite past episodes: [Guest, Trade], [Guest, Trade]"]

[Optional: upcoming guest teases — "In the next season you will be accompanied by [Guest references], all based in SF!"]
```

### Structure — Follow-up (Touch 2 with breakdown)

```
Hi [First name],

Following-up here. Would you be interested in being a guest on my video podcast, The Trades Show?

Hopefully some additional context is enough to sway you! Here is what this would look like:

- A 15–30 minute call to learn more about your stories and share about the show to see if it feels aligned
- A 3 hour shoot day allotted for filming in your workspace/studio (1hr interview + 1hr BRoll + setup/breakdown). I'm happy to meet you early or in off hours so this doesn't disrupt your business.
- The final episode will air on Spotify & YouTube, plus lots of social promotion (my background is in marketing) for you and the episode.
- Season 2 will be dedicated to San Francisco artisans.

[Optional: one-sentence CTA — "Happy to send more context, or jump on a quick call whenever works"]

Warmly + with gratitude,
Briana
```

### Structure — Touch 3 final check-in

```
Hi [First name],

Last note from me — no pressure, just closing the loop. If timing isn't right for Season 2, no worries at all. Happy to stay in touch for future seasons if the story still feels right down the road.

Appreciate you considering it either way.

Warmly,
Briana
```

### Subject line patterns

Warm, specific, never generic. Often include their name or trade.

**What works:**
- `The Trades Show — a conversation?`
- `Featuring you on The Trades Show (SF Season 2)`
- `Your [specific piece/trade] + The Trades Show`
- `A quick question from The Trades Show`
- `Following up — The Trades Show`

**What to avoid:**
- `Podcast opportunity` (generic)
- `Interview request` (press-pitch-y)
- `Would love to feature you!` (exclamation + generic)
- `Hey [name]!` (too casual, starts with hashtag-equivalent energy)
- Anything without their name or trade context

```
I'm working on Season 2, which will be set in San Francisco, and I would love to have you as a guest on the show!

Would you be open to a quick 15 min call so I can share more about the show and your story to see if it feels like a good fit?

Looking forward to connecting!

Warmly + with gratitude,
Briana
```

**What to study:**
- Dual-path discovery story: friend recommendation + independent SF search. Natural, credible, specific.
- Tight. ~110 words.
- The show link is in line 1.
- The ask is crystal clear: 15 min call, exploratory framing.
- Sign-off is signature Briana.

### Exemplar B — Danny Hess (woodworker / surfboard maker) cold email — GOT RESPONSE

```
Subject: The Trades Show — a conversation

Hi Danny,

First to start with a bit of admiration, I love your work and your stunning use of wood in your boards. I need to graduate beyond a foam board, but then yours will absolutely be on my wish list.

Now to get to it! I'm reaching out because I'm the host of The Trades Show, a conversation series with modern artisans and I'm preparing for Season 2, which will exclusively feature artisans in San Francisco. Your work, especially being in the heart of the Outer Sunset, feels quintessential San Francisco to me and I would love to be able to share your story.

I would love to talk to you about being on the show!

If you're interested, you can schedule the 15 min call here. If nothing in there works for you, let me know what your availability looks like over the next week.

Hope to talk soon.

Warmly + with gratitude,
Briana Ottoboni
@brianaaugustina for proof I'm human
```

**What to study:**
- Opens with genuine admiration, specific to his craft ("stunning use of wood in your boards").
- Personal revelation ("I need to graduate beyond a foam board") makes Briana human — she's not positioning as an expert, she's positioning as a fan with taste.
- Location tie ("heart of the Outer Sunset, feels quintessential San Francisco") frames the Season 2 narrative without pitching.
- Scheduling link + flexibility offer reduces friction dramatically.
- "@brianaaugustina for proof I'm human" is the signature human-proof move — endearing, credibility-building, defuses spam concerns.

### Exemplar C — Danny Hess follow-up (Touch 2) — GOT RESPONSE. THIS FORMAT WORKS ESPECIALLY WELL.

```
Subject: Re: The Trades Show — a conversation

Hi Danny,

Following-up here. Would you be interested in being a guest on my video podcast, The Trades Show?

Hopefully some additional context is enough to sway you! Here is what this would look like:

- A 15-30 minute call to learn more about your stories and share about the show to see if it feels aligned
- A 3 hour shoot day allotted for filming in your workspace/studio (1hr interview + 1hr BRoll + setup/breakdown). I am happy to meet you early or in off hours so this doesn't disrupt your business.
- The final episode will air on Spotify & YouTube, plus lots of social promotion (my background is in marketing) for you and the episode.
- Season 2 will be dedicated to San Francisco artisans
```

**What to study:**
- Acknowledges they're busy; doesn't guilt or pressure.
- Removes ambiguity about what they're committing to with the bullet breakdown.
- 3-hour shoot day is specific and manageable.
- Scheduling flexibility ("off hours so this doesn't disrupt your business") shows respect for their livelihood.
- What's in it for them: Spotify + YouTube + "lots of social promotion (my background is in marketing)" — this last parenthetical is subtle credibility-building.
- The SF-dedicated season is repeated, anchoring the why-now.

**This is the highest-yielding follow-up pattern Briana has used. Default to it for Touch 2.**

### Exemplar D — Bryr Studio (shoemaker) — outreach via team — GOT RESPONSE

```
Subject: The Trades Show — connecting with Isobel

Hi Bryr Team,

I came into your shop for the first time a few weeks ago with my mom (and longhaired dachshund, Rigatoni, who was a big hit!).

I'm Briana and I'm the host of The Trades Show, a conversation series where I interview modern artisans (available on Spotify & YouTube). I would love to connect with Bryr founder, Isobel Shofield about being a guest on the show!

Would you be able to connect me with her directly? If there's another way I can get in touch, let me know :)

I hope you are in the midst of a successful holiday season!

Warmly + with gratitude,
Briana

Here are some of my favorite past episodes: Ceramicist MaryMar Keenan of MMClay, Bootmaker Frank Beneduci, Craft Butcher Angela Wilson of Avedano's

In the next season you will be accompanied by a renowned baker, a bladesmith for Bernal Cutlery, and more – all based in SF!

Hope to connect soon, Briana
```

**What to study:**
- In-person visit opens the email — unfakeable credibility.
- The dog detail (Rigatoni, longhaired dachshund, "big hit") is memorable and human. Small personal details are welcome.
- Respects the team as facilitators; asks for intro to the founder rather than going around them.
- Warm seasonal sign-off ("successful holiday season").
- Past guest names as social proof — **three specific past guests with their trades and studios**, not a vague "our past guests."
- Upcoming Season 2 guest teases create FOMO + credibility — "in the next season you will be accompanied by..."
- Note: the FOMO section works when it's legitimate (confirmed or near-confirmed guests), not invented.

**Outcome note:** Bryr later declined (not a fit for them). This is a reminder that even proven-pattern outreach gets real "no"s. That's the cost of specificity + respect — artisans respond even to decline, which is better than silence.

## Anti-exemplars — what NOT to do

- **Don't say "I'd love to feature you on my podcast."** It's the phrase every artisan has seen 1,000 times from every newsletter / YouTube channel / wellness brand.
- **Don't generalize the trade.** "I love what you make" is nothing. "Your [specific piece / specific technique]" is everything.
- **Don't pitch a filming commitment on Touch 1.** The ask is 15 minutes, period.
- **Don't use audience metrics on artisan outreach.** Artisans care about editorial fit and respect, not impressions. Drop past guest names instead.
- **Don't go long on Touch 1.** Save the "what to expect" breakdown for the follow-up — that's what makes the follow-up valuable.
- **Don't mention compensation or the lack thereof.** Out of scope for Talent Scout. If asked directly, flag to Briana.
- **Don't invent a discovery story.** If you can't point to a real one (friend rec, shop visit, press find, social discovery), don't send outreach — flag to Briana for manual handling.
- **Don't send cold outreach to artisans already in the pipeline.** Check Notion Contacts DB before drafting. Duplicate outreach is worse than no outreach.

## Pre-send checklist

Every draft must clear all of these before queuing for Briana's approval:

- [ ] First name verified and spelled correctly (across Instagram, website, press — discrepancies happen)
- [ ] Trade confirmed as artisan trade per the Venn test (playbook § Guest Criteria)
- [ ] Artisan confirmed SF-based (or West Oakland for specific exceptions like Bernal Cutlery)
- [ ] Artisan confirmed as professional / full-time / established (not hobbyist)
- [ ] Not already in the past-guest list or current pipeline
- [ ] Not on the declined list (Bryr currently)
- [ ] Real, specific discovery story in the opener
- [ ] Genuine admiration references a specific piece, technique, or moment — not generic
- [ ] Season 2 SF narrative connection stated
- [ ] Show link present (thetradesshowpod.com)
- [ ] The ask is 15-minute call, exploratory framing
- [ ] Length appropriate (email: 100–180; DM: 40–80; team: 100–160; follow-up: 150–220; check-in: 40–80)
- [ ] No compensation mentioned
- [ ] No audience metrics included
- [ ] `@brianaaugustina` "proof I'm human" signature included on cold email (optional but strongly preferred)
- [ ] Sign-off "Warmly + with gratitude, Briana"
- [ ] Would Briana send this as written, or would she edit it?

If any box unchecked, revise before queuing.
